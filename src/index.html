Why Invest in Georgia?

Georgia is uniquely positioned at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, sharing land borders with Azerbaijan, Armenia, Turkey, and Russia. Its strategic location along the Black Sea provides access to significant trade routes, connecting through the Kerch Strait in the north to the Sea of Azov and, in the southwest, via the Bosphorus Strait to the Sea of Marmara and the Mediterranean Sea. This access extends further through the Mediterranean to the Atlantic Ocean, effectively linking Georgia with Russia, Romania, Bulgaria, Ukraine, and Turkey, enhancing its importance as a regional trade hub.

Business-Friendly Legislation

Georgia offers a favorable business environment, supported by laws that protect foreign investors.

100% Foreign Ownership Rights

Foreign investors can fully own businesses and assets, ensuring complete control and flexibility.

Inheritance Rights

The Constitution of Georgia recognizes and protects the right of inheritance for all investors.

WTO Membership

As a member of the World Trade Organization (WTO) since 2000, Georgia aligns with international trade standards, enhancing transparency and trust.

Generalized System of Preferences (GSP)

Georgia has established the GSP with the US, Canada, Switzerland, Japan, and Norway, enabling Georgian goods to enter these markets at reduced tariffs.

GSP with EU Countries

Georgia benefits from a GSP agreement with the European Union, allowing the export of over 7,200 products with customs exemptions.

Growing Exports to China

In 2021, Georgia's exports to China saw significant growth, making China its largest export market, as reported by the Georgian National Bureau of Statistics.

Tax and Customs Benefits

Exports and re-exports are exempt from VAT and customs tariffs, ensuring a cost-effective trade environment.

New Customs System

The country has modernized its customs procedures, further simplifying the movement of goods across borders.

Free Trade Agreements (FTA)

Georgia has established FTAs with countries such as Belarus, Ukraine, Azerbaijan, Armenia, Russia, Moldova, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, and Turkey, facilitating seamless trade within the region.

Deep and Comprehensive Free Trade Area (DCFTA)

A DCFTA agreement with EU countries, signed on June 27, 2014, enhances Georgia’s integration into European markets.

Skilled Workforce

Over 30% of the workforce holds a university degree, and the average monthly salary is below the European average, providing access to a highly educated yet cost-effective labor pool. Additionally, 50% of the unemployed population is between the ages of 20 and 34, representing a large pool of potential talent.

Open Trade Policies

Georgia has no trade restrictions with countries around the world and is committed to a free trade policy, supporting sustained economic growth and investment stability.

Growing Industrial Sector

The industrial sector plays a crucial role in the Georgian economy. In recent years, there has been substantial growth, with efforts to establish new factories and production facilities. Key industries include food and beverage packaging, which form a significant portion of Georgia’s industrial output.

Best Investments in Georgia

The best investment in Georgia, like in any country, can vary depending on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and investment horizon. Georgia has been attracting foreign investors in recent years due to its business-friendly environment, strategic location, and opportunities in various sectors. Here are some of the investment options that have been attractive in Georgia:

Real Estate

The real estate market in Georgia, particularly in the capital city of Tbilisi, has seen significant growth. Many investors have found success in buying and holding properties for rental income or capital appreciation. Georgia offers a simple and transparent property registration process for foreign investors.

Tourism and Hospitality

Georgia has become a popular tourist destination, and investments in hotels, resorts, and hospitality services can be lucrative, especially in areas like Tbilisi, Batumi, and the wine regions.

Agriculture

Agriculture is a key sector in Georgia, and there are opportunities for investments in the cultivation of crops, vineyards, and the production of wine and other agricultural products. The country has a favorable climate for agriculture.

Energy

Georgia has been investing in its energy infrastructure, including hydropower and renewable energy projects. Investing in energy production and distribution can be a profitable venture.

Technology and Startups

Georgia has a growing tech ecosystem, and investments in tech startups and IT services have been on the rise. Tbilisi, in particular, has a vibrant startup community.

Free Industrial Zones

Georgia has several free industrial zones that offer tax incentives and a favorable business environment for manufacturing and export-oriented industries.

Stock Market

Georgia has a stock exchange where you can invest in publicly traded companies. It's important to conduct thorough research and consult with financial experts before investing in individual stocks.

Bank Deposits and Bonds

Traditional investment options like bank deposits and government bonds can provide stability and predictable returns.

Before making any investment in Georgia or any other country, it's crucial to conduct due diligence, understand the local regulations, and seek advice from financial professionals. Consider your risk tolerance, investment horizon, and diversification strategy when making investment decisions.

Additionally, it's wise to stay updated on the economic and political developments in the country to make informed investment choices. Consulting with a local attorney or investment advisor can also be beneficial.
